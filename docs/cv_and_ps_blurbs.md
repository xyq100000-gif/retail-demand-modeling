# CV / PS / Contact Email Blurbs

## CV title

**Interpretable Retail Demand Modeling with Heterogeneity Controls and Time-Aware Validation | R**

## CV bullet option 1

- Reframed a grocery-sales coursework dataset into a reproducible panel demand-modeling project, comparing transformed-linear and count-based models under rolling and future-holdout evaluation.
- Showed that product/store heterogeneity controls materially improved forecasting accuracy and reduced overstated pooled promotion effects, while DISPLAY interactions added little out-of-sample value.

## CV bullet option 2

- Built interpretable count-regression pipelines for weekly store–product sales, incorporating seasonal controls, product/store fixed effects, and future-week validation.
- Demonstrated that validation design and heterogeneity structure can matter more than small interaction extensions when modeling overdispersed retail demand.

## PS sentence

In a retail-demand project, I found that the apparent value of promotion variables changed substantially once I aligned validation with future-week forecasting and controlled for product and store heterogeneity. That experience made me more careful about when pooled coefficients are informative and when they are mostly absorbing structural differences.
